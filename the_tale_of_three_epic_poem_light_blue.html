The Tale of Three: An Epic in Five Cantos

Canto I: The Transformation of Gregor Samsa

In a small, dim room, Gregor Samsa woke,

No longer man, but beast, his form bespoke

A monstrous insect, legs where limbs once grew,

His body now a horror, strange, askew.

Beneath the light of dawn, his terror rose,

The truth of his new shape, his deepest woes.

His life as salesman shattered by this curse,

He wondered if his fate could become worse.

Canto II: The Hunger Artist’s Lament

Within a cage, beneath the circus light,

The Hunger Artist sat in endless night.

He fasted long, while crowds would pass him by,

Indifferent to his struggle, cold and dry.

Where once the masses marveled at his feat,

Now none remained to watch him starve and beat.

A life devoted to his art of pain,

Misunderstood, his fast was all in vain.

Canto III: The Arrest of Josef K.

One dawn, Josef K. awoke in dread,

To boots that stomped with doom above his head.

No crime, no sin, had he to warrant chains,

Yet two men came with cold, indifferent reigns.

"Verhaftet? Für was? Welches Vergehen?"

He asked, but silence answered him again.

His ordered life, so carefully designed,

Was shattered by a law that he maligned.

Canto IV: The Silent Meeting of Fate

In shadows of despair, their spirits crossed—

Three souls whose lives were hopeless, bound, and lost.

Though never met in flesh, they knew the pain

Of alienation, faced with naught to gain.

Gregor, in his room, an insect still,

The Hunger Artist starving by sheer will,

And Josef K., pursued by silent fate,

All locked within their cages, all too late.

Canto V: The End of Three

And so it was that Gregor’s life expired,

His family, once dependent, now untired.

The door swung wide, revealing his remains,

A shell of horror lost in death’s domains.

His soul forgotten long before he died,

Relief, not sorrow, marked their final sighs.

Epilogue: The Forgotten Souls

In the end, their names were lost in time’s great flood,

Three lives erased in pain and quiet blood.

For Gregor, Artist, Josef—each a ghost—

Were overcome not by a single host,

But by the world’s indifference so vast,

And misunderstanding that forever lasts.
